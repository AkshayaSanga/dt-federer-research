# Methodology

## Objective

The objective of this assignment is to identify Hyderabad-based companies that match DeepThought’s Federer Company ICP: differentiated, promoter-led or founder-led, operationally active, systems-oriented, and working in manufacturing or deep-tech product sectors.

## City and Segment Selection

I selected Hyderabad as the target city because it has a strong concentration of aerospace, defence electronics, industrial IoT, robotics, precision engineering, and deep-tech manufacturing companies.

The selected segments are:

* Aerospace and precision engineering
* Defence electronics and embedded systems
* Industrial IoT and automation
* Robotics and mechatronics
* Deep-tech hardware and specialty manufacturing

## Research Sources Used

I used the following sources:

* Company websites
* F6S manufacturing startup directory
* LinkedIn company pages
* BSE and annual report pages where available
* News articles about facility expansion, contracts, funding, and product launches
* Public company descriptions and product pages

## Research Process

### Step 1: Initial Universe Building

I started with a broad list of Hyderabad manufacturing and deep-tech companies from public directories and startup databases. This included aerospace companies, industrial IoT companies, robotics companies, electronics companies, and specialty manufacturing startups.

### Step 2: Eligibility Filtering

Each company was first checked against two gates:

* E1 Producer: Does the company produce a product or deliver a specialized technical product/service in-house?
* E2 Accessible: Does the company have an operational presence in India, preferably Hyderabad?

Companies that were clearly software-only, trading-only, service-only, too large, or generic consumer businesses were rejected or marked borderline.

### Step 3: Federer Scoring

Companies were evaluated across six criteria:

* C3: Differentiation
* C4: Decision-maker quality
* C5: Growing sector
* C6: Growth signals
* C7: Systems maturity
* C8: Leadership succession

Each criterion was scored as Weak, Moderate, or Strong based on available evidence.

### Step 4: Evidence Collection

For each company, I looked for specific evidence such as:

* Technical products
* Aerospace or defence applications
* Certifications such as AS9100D or ISO
* New facilities or expansion
* Funding or contracts
* Founder or leadership background
* Export orientation
* Use of sensors, automation, AI, embedded systems, or advanced manufacturing

### Step 5: Final Classification

Companies were classified as:

* A: Strong Federer
* B: Probable Federer
* C: Borderline
* D: Not ICP

A-company and B-company profiles were prioritized for outreach. C-company profiles were kept as research leads that need more verification. D-company profiles were rejected.

## Key Learnings

The strongest Hyderabad companies were concentrated in aerospace, defence electronics, industrial IoT, robotics, and precision engineering.

The strongest signals of Federer fit were:

* In-house technical product capability
* Advanced manufacturing or embedded systems depth
* Facility expansion
* Export orientation
* Defence/aerospace certifications
* Founder-led or technical leadership
* Evidence of systems maturity

The weakest fits were companies that looked like manufacturing companies on directories but were actually software platforms, service providers, traders, or very early-stage ideas without visible operating infrastructure.
